How does food connect us to nature? The most obvious response to this question is because, simply put, most of the food we (should) eat comes directly from the natural world. Fruit bearing trees, vegetables, legumes and grains are all rooted in soil. If you eat animals, those animals will have consumed plants from the earth too. But before I start to sound like I’m teaching two year olds what vegetables are, the connection to nature through food goes a lot deeper than just how the food chain works.

By eating foods that we’re naturally meant to eat, we achieve adequate nourishment. The body needs 30 different vitamins and minerals (mostly from plants) and 9 different essential amino acids (from protein) as well as 3 essential fatty acids (Omega 3s). In order to have sufficient intake of all of these essential nutrients we need to eat a well balanced diet that is ideally made up of mostly or solely, whole plant foods, as recommended by a vast number of professionals in the field of nutrition.

Unfortunately the majority of our food comes from farms that degrade the land rather than resore it. Knowing where our food comes from is really important. A lot of us are so disconnected from nature that we don’t realise how our food is farmed. Most of us simply stroll into the supermarket and pick up ready prepared foods from the shelves. We don’t have to venture out into a forest to forage for berries or hunt anymore like our ancestors did. Everything is done for us by the food industry.

Another really great way to have more control over the quality of the food that you eat in terms of how it’s farmed, is to grow it yourself. Not everyone has the luxury of a garden, but sometimes all you need is a balcony or a windowsill. When you grow your own food you can choose not to use chemical fertilisers and pesticides. Instead you can choose to use compost and work with nature for pest control – Ladybirds are fantastic aphid police! When we make conscious food choices we will be healthy.
